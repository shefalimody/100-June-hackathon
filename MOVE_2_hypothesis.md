# Move 2 — The Hypothesis (pre-registered, before any code)

**Project:** Eval Co-pilot — Track B · 100x Cohort 7 Code Path Hackathon
**Committed before any application code is written.**

## The claim
A builder handed a **golden set** + a **rubric** for their own AI feature will find a **real
failure they did not already know about** — one they would have shipped — **more often than not.**

## The three numbers (pre-registered, out of 2 builders tested)

| Outcome | The number |
|---|---|
| **WIN — proves me right** | **1 or 2 of 2** builders find a real, previously-unknown failure they would have shipped (ideal: **2 of 2**) |
| **MIRROR — I only built a mirror** | **0 of 2** builders find anything new (the tool just confirms what they already knew) |
| **KILL-NUMBER — I admit I was wrong** | **0 of 2** builders find any new failure → it's a mirror, not an instrument |

## What counts
"Found a real failure" = something they did **not** already know **and** would have shipped.
"Flagged something they already knew" does **not** count.

## Honest note
Mirror risk with the careful fintech builder: he may already know his failure modes. If the tool
only confirms what he knew, that is an honest "mirror" finding — reported as-is, not fudged.
